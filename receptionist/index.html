AI Receptionist — Flagship Offer Brief

A flagship single-offer pitch for the home-services trades (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, roofing): an AI receptionist that answers calls, texts back missed ones, and books jobs. This is for partner discussion before going customer-facing — includes pricing variance from research, cost-to-serve reality check, the vertical-pivot decision, and the draft of what we'd actually pitch.

Drafted: May 2026 Status: Internal review Decision needed: Pricing anchor + vertical commitment

TL;DR

Three independent research agents converged on the same offer: an AI receptionist for home-services trades, leading with missed-call recovery + appointment booking. The category has visceral, dollar-quantified pain ($500–$1,200 lost per missed call), large unmet demand (42% of SMBs lose $500+/week to voicemail, only 22% have a solution), and a real defensibility window of ~24 months before voice AI commoditizes.

The strategic catch: our existing warm pipeline (water polo, salon, ski club, guide service, retail) is not in this vertical. Going with this offer means cold outreach into a fresh market.

Why this offer (research convergence)

I ran three parallel research agents from different angles. They independently picked the same answer:

Research lens Recommendation Pricing
Demand & willingness-to-pay "Never Miss Another Call" for plumbers/HVAC, PNW $1,500 setup + $497/mo
Speed-to-value Missed-call text-back + optional voice receptionist $497 setup + $297/mo
Whitespace & defensibility 24/7 AI Voice Receptionist, vertical-locked to home services $2,500–$5,000 install + $500–$1,200/mo

Strong convergence on vertical (home-services trades), category (AI receptionist / missed-call recovery), and differentiator (managed install + tuning + CRM integration, not the voice infra itself). Material disagreement only on pricing.

Pricing variance — needs a partner decision

The three agents recommended setup fees in a $497–$5,000 range and monthly subscriptions $297–$1,200. That's a 5x–10x spread. The decision is not "what's right" — it's "what do we anchor at for the first 30 days of cold pitches?" Each anchor has tradeoffs:

Anchor option Setup Monthly Tradeoff
Aggressive (low entry) $497 $297/mo Easiest cold close. Smallest revenue per customer. ~$4k/yr per account. Risks setting a low price anchor for word-of-mouth referrals.
Median (recommended starting point) $1,500 $497/mo Defensible against the dollar-quantified pain ($1,200/missed call). ~$7,500/yr per account. Matches what Agent A (the most detailed demand research) actually recommended.
Premium (managed-service framing) $3,500 $897/mo Closer to Smith.ai / Ruby Receptionists pricing band, positioned as a premium replacement. ~$14k/yr per account. Higher friction at cold pitch but qualifies prospects hard.
My pick: Median anchor for the 30-day pilot. $1,500 setup + $497/mo with a 30-day money-back guarantee on both. If the cold-pitch close rate is >15% at this price, push the next batch to $897/mo. If it's <5%, drop to $297/mo. The pilot data answers what reasoning can't.

Cost-to-serve reality check

Margins on voice AI are tighter than on chat. Quick math at typical usage (500 conversations/mo × ~3 min average):

  • Voice infra (Vapi / Retell / Synthflow / Twilio): ~$0.10–$0.20 per minute = $150–$300/mo per customer
  • LLM tokens (Claude Haiku for inference): ~$10–$25/mo per customer
  • SMS (A2P 10DLC + Twilio): ~$15/mo per customer
  • Our time at steady state: ~1 hour/mo per customer for tuning/issues = real cost depending on how we value our hours

Total cost-to-serve at the median price ($497/mo): roughly $200–$350/mo, gross margin 40–60%. That's healthy but tighter than the chat widget (90%+). Heavy users hitting the 500-cap can get expensive — we need a hard cap with overage at $1.00/conversation to protect margins.

The strategic question — vertical pivot

The research recommends targeting plumbers, HVAC techs, electricians, and roofers. None of our warm pipeline is in those trades. We have three paths:

Path Mechanic Risk / Reward
A. Full pivot to home services Cold outreach only — scrape PNW plumbing/HVAC numbers via Outscraper, run cold email + voicemail drops, hand off warm replies to the AI Strongest market signal. Sharper pitch. No warm leads, so conversion friction is higher initially.
B. Adapt the offer to warm pipeline Sell missed-call recovery to existing leads (salon, guide service, ski club) where calls also matter Higher conversion on warm leads (5–10× cold). Weaker pitch — pain isn't as visceral as a $1,200 missed plumbing job.
C. Run both as parallel pilots 5 cold pitches into home services + 5 warm pitches into existing pipeline. Compare conversion, deal size, install repeatability. Most data. Most effort. Recommended if we have bandwidth for 30 days of split focus.

My lean: Path C. The data from running both is worth more than the marginal effort, and we'll know within 30 days which channel actually pays.

How it works (3 steps)

1
It answers calls you miss.

Whether you're on another line, on a roof, or asleep — the AI picks up. Greets in your voice, asks what they need, texts you the lead or books the appointment directly.

2
It texts back missed callers automatically.

If a call goes to voicemail, the AI sends a real text within 30 seconds and starts a two-way conversation. Most "lost" leads are recovered before they call your competitor.

3
It books jobs into your calendar.

Once the customer commits to a time, the AI drops the appointment into Google Calendar, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, or Jobber. You walk into the day with the day already booked.

What's included (any deal)

  • 24/7 AI receptionist on the customer's existing phone number
  • Automatic missed-call text-back within 30 seconds
  • AI-powered SMS conversation that books appointments
  • Calendar integration (Google, ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro)
  • Voice trained on their business — services, pricing, hours, service area

  • Live takeover — they can jump in mid-conversation any time

Competitive framing (for the pitch)

  • vs. Smith.ai / Ruby Receptionists ($300–$700+/mo for human-staffed): 70% cheaper, 24/7 coverage, no scheduling gaps, books directly into the CRM.
  • vs. GoHighLevel / Tidio missed-call text-back ($49–$199/mo, self-install): we install and tune it; the customer never opens a settings panel.
  • vs. Vapi / Retell / Synthflow (DIY voice AI tools): we handle the SIP routing, prompt engineering, CRM wiring, and ongoing tuning. They sell the engine; we sell a working car.

Pressure-test items

  • A2P 10DLC SMS registration takes 3–5 days. If we go this direction, start the application before the first cold pitch. Otherwise it blocks the first install and we look amateur.
  • Number porting vs. call forwarding. Porting numbers is a customer-trust nightmare (week-long downtime risk). Standardize on call forwarding from day one — sidesteps the most common derail.
  • ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro could ship a native AI receptionist add-on in 12–24 months. Monitor their roadmaps. If/when it lands, our move is to either become their integration partner or pivot the offer sideways (estimate follow-up, review requests, win-back).
  • Margin compression risk. If Anthropic / OpenAI raise prices or deprecate cheap models, our 40–60% gross margin tightens. Stay model-agnostic in the backend.
  • Cold-outreach legal. Ringless voicemail and cold SMS have TCPA exposure. Review with a lawyer before we drop 500 numbers — class-action territory if done wrong.
  • Install scaling. The 90-minute install is fine at customer #1; if it inflates to 4+ hours by customer #20, the unit economics collapse. Codify the playbook before the third install.
  • One real customer's complaint can sink us. Voice AI mistakes (wrong quote, scheduling conflicts, hallucinated services) are visible and embarrassing. Heavy testing on the first 3 customers is non-negotiable.

30-day pilot path

1
Week 1 — Infrastructure setup.

A2P 10DLC application (3–5 day blocker). Twilio account + voice configuration. Codify the install playbook into a step-by-step checklist. Set up the public-facing landing page.

2
Week 2 — Outreach.

Scrape ~200 plumbing/HVAC phone numbers in PNW via Outscraper. Cold email + voicemail drop hybrid. Pitch: "I called you Tuesday at 4:47pm — your AI receptionist would have caught it." Drive replies to the landing page or direct booking link.

3
Week 3 — First installs.

Target 2–3 paying customers from outreach. Run installs against the playbook. Time everything. Capture failure modes and edit the playbook in real time.

4
Week 4 — Measure & decide.

Pull the pilot data: cold response rate, demo→close rate, install time per customer, gross margin per customer, qualitative feedback. Decide: scale up, adjust pricing, or kill.

Kill criteria

If we hit any of these by day 30, the offer is wrong as positioned and we pivot:

  • Cold outreach reply rate below 1% (something is off about the pitch or the channel)
  • Demo→close rate below 10% on warm replies (price or product is wrong)
  • Install time exceeds 4 hours per customer (playbook isn't repeatable)
  • Gross margin below 30% on first 3 customers (cost-to-serve is too high at this price)
