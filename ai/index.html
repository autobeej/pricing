AI Receptionist — Combined Flagship Brief

A single product across two channels — an embeddable AI chat widget on the customer's website plus automatic missed-call text-back on their phone line. Both powered by the same managed RAG knowledge base. Voice AI deferred for a later release. This brief consolidates the AI chat widget research and the AI receptionist research into one offer for partner discussion.

Drafted: May 2026 Status: Internal review Decision needed: Pricing anchor + vertical commitment

TL;DR

One product, two channels, one knowledge base. Web visitors get the AI chat widget; phone callers who hit voicemail get an AI text conversation within 30 seconds. The customer brings their content — services, pricing, hours, FAQs — and we install, tune, and run both channels as a managed monthly subscription.

Three independent research agents converged on this category as the right flagship: visceral, dollar-quantified pain (especially for service businesses), large unmet demand, ~24-month defensibility window. The strategic catch is that the strongest target vertical (home-services trades) is not in our existing warm pipeline. Voice AI is intentionally out of scope for v1 — chat + SMS-text-back covers the highest-ROI channels at much better margins, and we add voice as a Pro+ upgrade once the core product is selling.

The Product

One unified AI agent, deployed across two channels:

  1. Web chat widget. One-line script tag drops onto any existing website. Visitors chat with the AI; it answers questions, captures leads, books meetings, escalates to a human when needed.
  2. Missed-call text-back. When a phone call goes to voicemail, the AI sends an SMS within 30 seconds and starts a real two-way conversation. Customers who'd otherwise have called the next vendor stay engaged.

The same RAG knowledge base powers both — customer uploads docs (PDFs, Google Docs), pastes their FAQ, or we crawl their existing site. The same configurable agent personality runs across web and SMS so the customer's brand voice is consistent everywhere.

What we sell vs. what's commoditized: the underlying widget infra, LLM APIs, and SMS routing are all commoditized building blocks. Our offer is the install and tune and manage layer — we ingest the knowledge, write the system prompt, calibrate tone, wire up lead routing, and keep it working. That service layer is what justifies the price band.

Cost-to-serve reality check

Margins are healthy without voice. At Standard tier (the recommended anchor):

  • LLM tokens (Claude Haiku / GPT-4o-mini): ~$5/mo per customer at 300 conversations
  • SMS via Twilio (A2P 10DLC): ~$15/mo per customer (assumes 50 missed-call texts × multi-message conversations)
  • A2P 10DLC carrier fees: ~$2/mo per customer
  • Hosting / infra overhead (amortized): ~$3/mo per customer
  • Total cost-to-serve: ~$25/mo
  • Gross margin at $149/mo: ~83%

Hook is even better (~92%); Pro is ~85% with heavy usage. The unit economics are strong because we're staying out of voice-infra costs.

The strategic question — vertical commitment

The research recommended targeting home-services trades (plumbers, HVAC, electricians, roofers) because that's where missed-call pain is most visceral and most quantifiable. Our existing warm pipeline (water polo nonprofit, salon, ski club, guide service, retail brand) is mostly outside that vertical. Three paths:

PathMechanicTradeoff
A. Full pivot to home services Cold outreach only — scrape PNW plumbing/HVAC numbers via Outscraper, run cold email + voicemail drops, route warm replies to the AI Strongest market signal, sharpest pitch ($1,200/missed call). No warm leads, higher acquisition friction initially.
B. Adapt to warm pipeline Pitch chat + missed-call text-back to existing leads (salon, guide service, ski club) where calls also matter 5–10× higher conversion on warm leads. Weaker pitch — pain isn't as visceral or as countable.
C. Run both as parallel pilots · recommended 5 cold pitches into home services + 5 warm pitches into existing pipeline. Compare conversion, deal size, install repeatability over 30 days. Most data, most effort. The comparison is worth more than what it costs to run both.

Competitive framing

  • vs. Tidio / Crisp / Chatra ($19–$49/mo): they sell a self-installed widget; we install it, tune the agent on the customer's content, and run it as a service.
  • vs. Intercom Fin ($0.99 per resolution + $75+/mo Intercom): we're flat-fee and predictable; Intercom adds up fast at any volume.
  • vs. Chatbase / Voiceflow / build-your-own ($29–$399/mo): the customer does all the work themselves on those platforms. We do the work for them.
  • vs. GoHighLevel-style missed-call text-back ($49–$199/mo): their text-back is a static auto-reply. Ours is a real two-way AI conversation that books the appointment.
  • vs. Smith.ai / Ruby Receptionists ($300–$700/mo human-staffed): 50–80% cheaper, 24/7 with no scheduling gaps, no minimum-call commitment. We're not voice yet — that's coming — but for chat + SMS we're well below their floor.

Pressure-test items

  • A2P 10DLC SMS registration takes 3–5 days. Start the application before the first cold pitch — otherwise it blocks the first install and we look amateur.
  • TCPA / cold-outreach legal exposure. Ringless voicemail and cold SMS have real liability under US TCPA. Review with an attorney before scraping any list — class-action territory if done wrong.
  • Differentiation against free / freemium alternatives. "We install it for you" works against pure DIY tools but only persuades buyers who don't want to DIY. Lead the pitch with the install + tune + run service, not the widget itself.
  • Install scaling. 1-hour install at customer #1 is fine; if it inflates to 4+ hours by customer #20, unit economics collapse. Codify the playbook into a checklist before customer #3.
  • Token-model dependency. If Anthropic / OpenAI raise prices or deprecate cheap models, our 80%+ margin tightens. Stay model-agnostic in the backend.
  • One bad demo can sink us. AI mistakes (wrong quote, bad scheduling, hallucinated services) are visible and embarrassing. Heavy testing on the first 3 customers is non-negotiable.
  • ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro could ship native AI add-ons in 12–24 months. Monitor their roadmaps. Mitigation is to become an integration partner, not a competitor.

30-day pilot path

1
Week 1 — Infrastructure setup.

A2P 10DLC application (3–5 day blocker). Twilio account + SMS number. Codify the install playbook into a step-by-step checklist. Stand up a public-facing landing page at a sub-route.

2
Week 2 — Outreach (Path C: parallel).

5 cold pitches into PNW home-services trades (scraped Outscraper list). 5 warm pitches into existing pipeline (Sweet Spot, Divinity, ski club, etc.). Same Standard tier offer, same money-back guarantee. Track which channel converts.

3
Week 3 — First installs.

Target 2–3 paying customers from the warm + cold mix. Run installs against the playbook. Time everything. Capture failure modes and edit the playbook in real time.

4
Week 4 — Measure & decide.

Pull the pilot data: cold response rate, demo→close rate, install time per customer, gross margin, qualitative feedback. Decide: scale up which channel, adjust pricing, or kill.

Kill criteria

If we hit any of these by day 30, the offer is wrong as positioned and we pivot:

  • Cold outreach reply rate below 1% (something is off about the pitch or the channel)
  • Demo→close rate below 10% on warm replies (price or product is wrong)
  • Install time exceeds 4 hours per customer (playbook isn't repeatable)
  • Gross margin below 60% on first 3 customers (cost-to-serve is too high at this price)

Future — Voice AI as a Pro+ tier

Once the chat + SMS product is selling, voice receptionist becomes the natural next-tier upgrade. The infra (Vapi / Retell / Synthflow + Twilio + same RAG) is straightforward to add; the commercial upside is high (the human-staffed receptionist incumbents charge $300–$700/mo). Likely positioning when we do launch it: Pro+ Voice at $499–$799/mo with a $1,500 setup. Triggers to start building voice: 10+ paying chat/SMS customers, install playbook stable, <4 hours per install. Targets to hit before voice launch: 50% of Pro customers asking about voice unprompted.
